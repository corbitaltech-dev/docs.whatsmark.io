Custom fields let you store your own details on every contact, beyond name and phone number. For example: "Company Name", "Contract Value", "Preferred Language", or "Renewal Date".

Once you create a field, it appears on the **Custom Fields** tab whenever you add or edit a contact. You can also show it as a column in your contact list and use it to personalize messages.

## Creating a custom field

<BrowserFrame
  src="/core-setup/img/custom field-form.png"
  url="whatsmark.io/setup/custom-fields"
  alt="Custom Field form showing field name, type selector, and field options"
  caption="Image shows the New Custom Field form."
/>

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Setup → Custom Fields">
  Click **Setup** in the left menu, then **Custom Fields**.
</Step>

<Step title="Click Create Custom Field">
  A form opens on the right side of the screen. It has two tabs: **Basic** and **Options**.
</Step>

<Step title="Fill in the Basic tab">
  Enter a **Label** — this is the field name your team will see, such as "Company Name". Then pick a **Type** that matches the data, such as Text, Number, or Date.

  The other fields on this tab are optional. Each one is explained below.
</Step>

<Step title="Click Next to open the Options tab">
  If you chose **Dropdown**, **Radio**, or **Checkbox**, add the choices here. You must add at least one choice before saving.

  For other types, this tab lets you set optional limits, such as a minimum and maximum for numbers or an earliest and latest date.
</Step>

<Step title="Click Save">
  Your field is ready. It now appears on the **Custom Fields** tab whenever you add or edit a contact.
</Step>
</Steps>

## What each field means

**Label**

The field name your team sees, such as "Company Name". Required.

**Slug**

A short internal name. WhatsMark.io fills it in for you from the Label — you can leave it as is. It cannot be changed after saving.

**Type**

The kind of data this field stores. See the table below.

**Default Value**

A value filled in automatically for new contacts. Optional.

**Placeholder**

Hint text shown inside the empty field, such as "Enter company name". Optional.

**Description**

Help text shown below the field to guide your team. Optional.

**Sort Order**

Controls the position of this field when you have many custom fields. Lower numbers appear first.

**Required**

Turn this on to make the field compulsory. A contact cannot be saved until it is filled.

**Show in Table**

Turn this on to show the field as a column in your contact list.

<Note>
Make a field **Required** only if every contact truly needs it. Once it is required, you must fill it every time you save any contact.
</Note>

## Field types

![Type dropdown open on the New Custom Field form, listing Text, Text Area, Number, Date, Email, URL, Phone, Dropdown, Radio, Checkbox, Toggle, Color, and Time](/core-setup/img/custom-field-types.png)

Pick the type that matches your data:

| Type | Use it for | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Text | A short line of text | Company Name |
| Text Area | Longer text over many lines | Special instructions |
| Email | An email address | Billing email |
| URL | A website link | Company website |
| Phone | A phone number | Office number |
| Number | A number | Contract value |
| Dropdown | Pick one choice from a list | Industry |
| Radio | Pick one choice, all shown at once | Preferred language |
| Checkbox | Pick more than one choice | Interested products |
| Date | A calendar date | Renewal date |
| Time | A time of day | Preferred call time |
| Color | A color | Brand color |
| Toggle | A simple yes or no | VIP customer |

## Options for each type

When you click **Next**, the Options tab changes based on the type you picked:

- **Dropdown, Radio, Checkbox** — add your choices here. At least one choice is required.
- **Text, Text Area** — set a minimum and maximum length. Optional.
- **Number** — set a minimum and maximum value. Optional.
- **Date** — set the earliest and latest date allowed. Optional.
- **Checkbox** — also set how many choices must be picked. Optional.
- All other types — nothing to set here. Just click **Save**.

## Editing and managing fields

Your custom fields appear in a list on the same page. From here you can:

- **Edit** — open the row menu and click **Edit** to change the label, type, or options. The Slug cannot be changed.
- **Turn on or off** — use the **Active** toggle on each row. An inactive field is hidden from the contact form and from message personalization, but the saved values are kept. Turn it back on and everything returns.
- **Delete** — open the row menu and click **Delete** to remove the field everywhere.

<Warning>
Deleting a custom field cannot be undone. The field disappears from every contact. If you are not sure, turn the field off with the **Active** toggle instead.
</Warning>

## Where custom fields appear

After you create a field, it works across WhatsMark.io:

- On the **Custom Fields** tab when you [add or edit a contact](/contacts/managing-contacts).
- As a column in your contact list, if **Show in Table** is on.
- As a personalization token in messages and campaigns — you pick it from the token list, no typing needed. See [Merge Fields](/messaging/merge-fields).
- When [importing contacts](/contacts/import-export), you can match a column in your file to a custom field.
- When a customer fills a [WhatsApp Flow](/automation/whatsapp-flows), their answers can be saved into custom fields.

## Tips

- Create your custom fields before importing contacts, so you can fill them during the import.
- Use **Dropdown** instead of **Text** when the answers come from a fixed list. It keeps the data clean.
- Turn on **Show in Table** only for the fields you check often, so your contact list stays easy to read.
