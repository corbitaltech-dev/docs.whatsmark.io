Every workspace starts with just its owner (the person who registered it). Go to **Team** to bring in the rest of your team, and **Roles** if you need more control than the default roles give you.

## Inviting your team

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Team link">
  This page lists everyone with access to your workspace — their role and status — plus any pending invitations.
</Step>

<Step title="Invite by email">
  Enter the email address of the person you want to invite and assign them a role.
</Step>

<Step title="Track the invitation">
  Pending invitations stay listed until accepted. You can revoke an invitation at any time before it's accepted.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
The number of team members you can have is limited by your plan.
</Warning>

Once someone accepts, you can:

- Open their **member detail page** to review their role and activity.
- **Remove** them from the workspace.
- **Change their role** — reassigning roles is restricted to the workspace owner.

<Info>
Curious what the invited person sees? See [Accepting a Team Invitation](/core-setup/invitation-acceptance) for their side of the flow.
</Info>

## Roles & Permissions

By default, every workspace has an **Owner** role plus standard **Admin**/**Member**-style roles. If you need finer control, go to **Setup → Roles** to create **custom roles**.

<Steps>
<Step title="Create a custom role">
  Give the role a name, then work through the permission matrix.
</Step>

<Step title="Set permissions per module">
  Each module (Contacts, Channels, Campaigns, Flows, Chat, Billing, and more) gets its own **View / Create / Edit / Delete** checkboxes, so you can grant exactly the level of access a role needs.
</Step>

<Step title="Assign it to team members">
  Once saved, the custom role becomes available in the role dropdown when inviting or editing a team member.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Granting any capability (Create/Edit/Delete) on a module automatically implies View access for that module — you can't give someone Edit rights without also letting them see the page.
</Note>

<Tip>
Only the workspace owner can reassign roles that affect ownership-level privileges — this keeps a single source of truth for who ultimately controls the workspace.
</Tip>
