At first you are the only person in your workspace. Add everyone else who works with you from this page.

## What you see on the screen

The page has two parts.

**Team Members** lists everyone in your workspace:

- **Name** — click it to open their profile.
- **Email** — the address they sign in with.
- **System Role** — the role they have been given.
- **Joined** — when they were added.
- **Last Seen** — the last time they used the workspace.

**Pending Invitations** only appears when you have invited a team member who has not signed up yet.

In the top corner you also see how many team members you have used, and a **Manage Roles** button that takes you to [Roles & Permissions](/core-setup/roles-and-permissions).

## Adding a team member

You can add them in two ways.

### Create the account yourself

Click **Create Member**. You fill in their details and pick their role, then they can sign in straight away.

![The Add Member form with fields for name, email, WhatsApp number and password](/core-setup/img/create-member.png)

First their details:

- **Name** — their full name.
- **Email** — they sign in with this, and it should be unique.
- **WhatsApp number** — should also be unique. Each member needs their own number.
- **Password** and **Confirm Password** — at least 8 characters. Use **Generate** if you would rather not think of one.

Then click **Next** to choose what they can do. Either turn on **Is Admin**, or pick a **Role** from the list. Once you pick a role you can see exactly what it allows.

### Invite them by email

Click **Invite Member** instead, and they set up their own account.

![The Invite Team Member form with an email field and a role picker](/core-setup/img/invite-member.png)

Enter their **email address** and pick the **role** they should have. You can see what that role allows before you send it. They get an email with a link, and they choose their own name, WhatsApp number and password. See [Accepting a Team Invitation](/core-setup/invitation-acceptance) for what that looks like from their side.

<Note>
The invite link works for **7 days**. After that you need to send a new one.
</Note>

## Pending invitations

Anyone you have invited who has not signed up yet sits in the **Pending Invitations** list, with the role they were given and when it was sent.

**Revoke** cancels the invitation, and their link stops working right away.

There is no resend button. If they need another email, revoke the old invitation first and then invite them again.

## Owners, admins and members

There are three levels, and the difference matters.

- **Owner** is the account that created the workspace. There is only one, and it cannot be changed here.
- **Admin** is anyone with the **Is Admin** switch turned on.
- **Member** is everyone else, and their role decides what they can do.

<Warning>
**Owners and admins ignore roles completely.** They can see and do everything. Only make a member an admin if you are happy for them to have full access.
</Warning>

Only the workspace owner can make a member an admin.

## Extra permissions for one member

Sometimes one member needs a little more than their role gives them, and you do not want a whole new role for it.

When you pick a role while adding or editing a member, you can select extra permission beyond what the role includes. Those extras apply to that member only, and everyone else with the same role is unaffected.

## Editing and removing members

Click a member's name to open their profile, or use the menu at the end of their row.

You can change their role at any time. You cannot edit yourself here, use your own profile settings for that and the owner cannot be edited at all.

<Warning>
When you remove a member, their contacts and chats are not given to anyone else. Give their contacts and chats to another member first.
</Warning>

## Team member limits

Your plan sets how many members can be in your workspace. The card at the top of the page shows how many of those you have used.

Pending invitations count too, because each one becomes a member when it is accepted. If you are out of room, revoking an invitation frees a space. You can also upgrade your plan or buy extra seats.
