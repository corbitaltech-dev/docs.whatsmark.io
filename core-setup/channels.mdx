Nothing sends or receives on WhatsApp or Messenger until you connect at least one **Channel**. All of your connected accounts — across both platforms — live on a single page: **Settings → Channels**.

<Info>
This page expands on the quick version covered in [Getting Started](/getting-started#connect-a-whatsapp-business-account). Read this page for the full picture, including manual connection, channel health, and business profile management.
</Info>

## The Channels page

The Channels page lists every messaging account connected to your workspace — WhatsApp Business Accounts (WABAs) and Facebook Pages (Messenger) — in one unified table, with each row showing its phone number or handle, platform, and connection status.

<Warning>
The number of channels you can connect is limited by your plan (`channel_accounts`). Free-plan workspaces can connect up to 2 channels; Professional plans raise this to 10; Reseller plans are unlimited. Once you hit your limit, connecting a new channel is blocked until you disconnect one or upgrade.
</Warning>

## Connecting WhatsApp

<Steps>
<Step title="Click Connect">
  From the Channels page, click **Connect** and choose **WhatsApp** from the platform picker.
</Step>

<Step title="Pick a connection method">
  You'll see two tabs:

  - **Embedded (recommended)** — WhatsMark uses Meta's official **Embedded Signup** flow. A Facebook login popup opens; log into your Facebook Business account, choose or create a WhatsApp Business Account and phone number, and confirm. The popup closes automatically once complete.
  - **Manual** — for advanced setups where you already have WhatsApp Business API credentials. Paste in your existing **WABA ID**, **access token**, **Meta App ID**, and **App Secret** directly.
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm it's connected">
  Once connected, the channel appears in your Channels list with a **Connected** status, and a webhook subscription is set up automatically so incoming messages reach your workspace.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Both connection methods can be individually disabled by your plan (`emb_signup`, `manual_channel`). If a tab is missing, that method isn't available on your current plan.
</Note>

## Connecting Messenger

Messenger connects the same way — from the platform picker, choose **Messenger** instead of WhatsApp, then pick **Embedded** (one-click Facebook Page login) or **Manual** (paste in a Page access token).

<Tip>
You can connect more than one channel of each platform — for example, several WhatsApp numbers, or multiple Facebook Pages — and manage all of them from the same Channels page.
</Tip>

## Coexistence mode

If enabled on your plan (`coexistence`), you can connect a WhatsApp number in **coexistence mode** — this keeps your existing WhatsApp Business app active on your phone alongside the API connection. Chat history and contacts from the app are imported, and messages stay in sync between the app and WhatsMark.

## Managing a connected channel

Open a channel from the list to:

- **Set it as the default** endpoint for its platform (used when a feature doesn't ask you to pick a channel explicitly).
- **Refresh** its health status and **view webhook subscription status** — re-subscribe if it's ever out of sync.
- **Update missing app credentials**, if Meta requires you to re-enter them.
- **Send a test message** to confirm the connection actually works end-to-end.
- **Disconnect** the whole account, or just a single phone number/endpoint under it.
- View **WABA health** — quality rating, review status, business verification, and conversation analytics (WhatsApp only).

## Business Profile

For WhatsApp channels, open the channel and go to its **Profile** tab to edit the public WhatsApp Business Profile your contacts see: about text, address, description, email, websites, business vertical/category, and profile photo.

<Info>
If a platform isn't available on your plan at all, the Channels page still shows it as a locked row with an upgrade prompt, rather than hiding it entirely.
</Info>
