Set up the building blocks you'll use to organize and categorize your contacts. These settings live under **Setup → Contacts** and are the same five tabs you see when managing contacts.

<Tip>
You don't have to set these up before adding your first contact — but setting them up early means every contact you create afterward can be organized correctly from the start.
</Tip>

## Labels

Freeform tags you can attach to any contact (e.g. "VIP", "Needs follow-up"). Create, edit, delete, and drag to reorder — the order here is the order they appear in throughout the product.

## Groups

Named collections of contacts, used for manual segmentation and as an audience option when creating a [Campaign](/messaging/campaigns). Create, edit, and delete groups; add or remove members from the Contacts page itself.

## Statuses

Represents where a contact is in your lifecycle (e.g. "New", "Contacted", "Customer"). Create, edit, delete, and reorder statuses, and mark one as the **default** status assigned to new contacts.

## Sources

Tracks where a contact came from (e.g. "Website", "Referral", "Ad Campaign"). Create, edit, delete, and reorder — useful for the dashboard's contact-source breakdown chart.

## Custom Fields

Add your own fields to the contact record beyond the built-in ones. Each custom field has:

- A **type**: text, dropdown, radio, checkbox, switch, or date.
- Type-specific **options/settings** (e.g. the list of choices for a dropdown).
- An **active/inactive** toggle — inactive fields are hidden from forms but keep their stored data.
- Drag-to-reorder, same as labels/statuses/sources.

<Note>
Custom fields you create here immediately become available as merge-field tokens (see [Merge Fields](/messaging/merge-fields)) and as mapping targets when importing contacts or mapping [WhatsApp Flow submissions](/automation/whatsapp-flows).
</Note>
