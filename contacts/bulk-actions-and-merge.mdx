Once you have contacts in your workspace, you often need to change many of them at once — or clean up duplicates. Both are available directly from the Contacts table.

## Bulk actions

Select one or more contacts (checkboxes on each row), or use **Select all matching** to target every contact matching your current filters — even ones not shown on the current page. A bar appears above the table with **Merge** and **Bulk Actions**.

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/bulk-selection-bar.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="Contacts table with rows selected and a bar above it reading 15 selected, with a link to select all contacts matching current filters, plus Merge and Bulk Actions buttons and a Clear option"
  caption="Selecting contacts reveals the action bar. Use the link beside the count to extend your selection to every contact matching your filters."
/>

Choosing **Bulk Actions** opens a sheet where you set only the fields you want to change — anything left on **No change** is untouched, and you can combine several changes in one go:

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/bulk-actions-sheet.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="Bulk Actions sheet for 15 selected contacts with a highlighted Delete selected contacts option, and dropdowns each defaulting to No change for status, type, group, label, assignee, active status, and linking or unlinking a WhatsApp channel"
  caption="Every dropdown defaults to No change, so you only touch what you choose. Delete stands apart and can't be combined with other changes."
/>

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Change status / change type">
  Move the selected contacts to a different lifecycle status, or change their contact type.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Assign to a team member">
  Bulk-assign ownership of the selected contacts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Add / remove group">
  Add the selection to a group, or remove them from one.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Add / remove label">
  Same idea, for labels.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Activate / deactivate">
  Toggle whether the selected contacts are active.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Link / unlink channel">
  **Link to WhatsApp channel** adds a channel without removing any the contacts are already linked to. **Unlink from WhatsApp channel** pairs with it to correct a wrong channel — for example, after an import that used the wrong one.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Delete">
  Permanently remove the selected contacts. Delete **can't be combined with other changes**, and requires a separate delete permission, since it's irreversible.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
Deleting contacts in bulk cannot be undone. Double-check your filters before confirming a bulk delete, especially when using "Select all matching."
</Warning>


## Merging duplicate contacts

If the same person ends up as two or more contact records (for example, they messaged from two numbers, or were imported twice), you can fold them together.

### Merging a single duplicate

<Steps>
<Step title="Find the duplicate">
  Use **Merge with...** from a contact's row menu.
</Step>

<Step title="Choose the primary contact">
  The **primary** is the record that survives — the other folds into it.
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm">
  The duplicate's data is merged into the primary, and the duplicate record is marked as merged.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Bulk merge

You can also select **multiple** contacts at once (2–100), choose one as the primary, and fold them all into it in a single action. If any contact in the selection fails to merge, the whole operation is rolled back — you'll never end up with a partial merge.

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/bulk-merge.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="Merge selected contacts dialog explaining that the other contacts will fold into the primary, with a radio list of the selected contacts and the first marked Primary"
  caption="Pick which record survives. Identities, notes, and chats from every channel are preserved on the primary."
/>

<Note>
Merging fills in the gaps rather than overwriting: blank fields on the primary are filled from the duplicate, and groups and labels from both are combined. A populated field on the primary is never replaced.
</Note>

<Tip>
A contact that has already been merged into another one can't be merged again — merges are one-directional and final.
</Tip>
