Once you have contacts in your workspace, you often need to change many of them at once — or clean up duplicates. Both are available directly from the Contacts table.

## Bulk actions

Select one or more contacts (checkboxes on each row), or use **Select all matching** to target every contact matching your current filters — even ones not shown on the current page. Then choose an action:

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Change status / change type">
  Move the selected contacts to a different lifecycle status, or change their contact type.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Assign to a team member">
  Bulk-assign ownership of the selected contacts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Add / remove group">
  Add the selection to a group, or remove them from one.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Add / remove label">
  Same idea, for labels.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Activate / deactivate">
  Toggle whether the selected contacts are active.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Link / unlink channel">
  Associate (or remove the association) between the selection and a specific WhatsApp/Messenger channel.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Delete">
  Permanently remove the selected contacts. This requires a separate delete permission from other bulk actions, since it's irreversible.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
Deleting contacts in bulk cannot be undone. Double-check your filters before confirming a bulk delete, especially when using "Select all matching."
</Warning>


## Merging duplicate contacts

If the same person ends up as two or more contact records (for example, they messaged from two numbers, or were imported twice), you can fold them together.

### Merging a single duplicate

<Steps>
<Step title="Open the contact you want to keep">
  This becomes the "winner" — the record that survives.
</Step>

<Step title="Choose Merge">
  Pick the duplicate ("loser") contact to fold into it.
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm">
  The loser's data is merged into the winner, and the loser record is marked as merged.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Bulk merge

You can also select a winner and **multiple** loser contacts at once (2–100), and merge them all into that one winner in a single action. If any contact in the selection fails to merge, the whole operation is rolled back — you'll never end up with a partial merge.

<Tip>
A contact that has already been merged into another one can't be used again as a winner or a loser — merges are one-directional and final.
</Tip>
