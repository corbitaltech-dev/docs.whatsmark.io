The **Contacts** page is where every person who has messaged your business — or that you've added manually — lives. It's the hub you'll come back to most often.

<Tip>
Before adding contacts in bulk, it's worth setting up [Contact Configuration](/core-setup/contact-configuration) first — labels, groups, statuses, sources, and custom fields — so every contact you create can be organized correctly from the start.
</Tip>

## Browsing contacts

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/contacts-list-filters.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="Contacts table with the Filters panel open showing Type, Status, Source, Active, Assignee, and Channel dropdowns, above rows listing each contact's type, status, source, assignee, and channel"
  caption="Open Filters to narrow the table. Quick tabs across the top jump straight to Leads, Customers, or Guests."
/>

The Contacts page shows a paginated, searchable table of every contact in your workspace. Filter by:

- **Type**
- **Status**
- **Source**
- **Assigned to** (team member)
- **Channel**

<Warning>
The total number of contacts you can store is limited by your plan. If you're near your limit, the dashboard's Usage panel shows a warning, and you can purchase a **contact-limit add-on** from [Billing](/billing/addons-and-upgrades) instead of upgrading your whole plan.
</Warning>

## Creating and editing a contact

Click **Create Contact** to open the contact form in a side sheet:

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/contact-form.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="New Contact form with Details and Custom Fields tabs, showing fields for first and last name, phone, email, type, assigned to, status, source, WhatsApp channel, display name, groups, and labels"
  caption="Required fields are marked with an asterisk. The + buttons let you create a status, source, group, or label without leaving the form."
/>

<Steps>
<Step title="Fill in the basics">
  Name, phone number, and email.
</Step>

<Step title="Set organization fields">
  Assign a lifecycle **status**, **source**, one or more **groups** and **labels**, and optionally assign the contact to a team member.
</Step>

<Step title="Fill in custom fields">
  Any [custom fields](/core-setup/contact-configuration#custom-fields) you've defined appear here too.
</Step>

<Step title="Save">
  The contact is added immediately and appears in the table.
</Step>
</Steps>

Editing an existing contact opens the same form, pre-filled.

## The contact detail view

Click any contact's name to open a read-only detail sheet showing their full activity history, notes, and custom field values.

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/contact-detail.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="Contact detail sheet with Contact Details and Notes tabs, showing the contact's type and labels, source, assignee, last message and created times, groups, and linked message channels"
  caption="The detail sheet opens over the table, so you can check a contact without losing your place."
/>

### Notes

From the detail view, add freeform notes to a contact — useful for logging context that shouldn't go out as a message. Notes can be edited or deleted at any time.

## Row actions

Each contact row has a menu of quick actions:

<BrowserFrame
  src="/contacts/img/contact-row-actions.png"
  url="app.whatsmark.io/contacts"
  alt="Contacts table with a row menu open listing View, Edit, Open chat, Initiate chat, Send flow, Merge with, and Delete"
  caption="The actions available depend on the contact — chat options only appear for contacts you can message on WhatsApp."
/>

- **View** — open the contact's detail sheet.
- **Edit** — open the contact form, pre-filled.
- **Open chat** (only shown if this contact already has a conversation thread) — jump straight to this contact's conversation thread.
- **Initiate chat** (only shown for contacts sourced from WhatsApp) — send a one-off WhatsApp template message to this contact, even outside the normal chat flow. See [Live Chat](/messaging/live-chat) for how this fits into the 24-hour messaging window.
- **Send flow** (only shown for contacts sourced from WhatsApp) — trigger one of your [WhatsApp Flows](/automation/whatsapp-flows) for this contact.
- **Merge with** — combine this contact with a duplicate. See [Bulk Actions & Merge](/contacts/bulk-actions-and-merge).
- **Activate / Deactivate** — toggle whether the contact is active without deleting them.
- **Delete** — permanently remove the contact.
