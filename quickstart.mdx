## Step 1: Create your free workspace

The Free plan costs nothing, never expires, and is enough to send your first message. You can move to a paid plan later if you outgrow it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick your plan">
    Go to the [WhatsMark pricing page](https://whatsmark.io/pricing) and choose **Free**. You pick your plan before signing up, so this is where everyone starts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your account">
    Enter your name, email, password and WhatsApp number. We'll send a code to that number to verify it's yours.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name your workspace">
    Choose a workspace name and a subdomain your team will sign in at something like `yourcompany.whatsmark.io`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait a moment while we set things up">
    Your workspace is created in the background, then you're signed in automatically and land on your dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Chose **Professional** instead? You'll be asked to pay before you can use the workspace. See [Free vs. Paid Plans](/getting-started/free-vs-paid) for what each plan includes.
</Note>

Full walkthrough: [Register](/getting-started/register).

## Step 2: Connect your WhatsApp number

Nothing sends or receives until you connect a number, so start here.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Channels and click Connect Channel">
    **Channels** is where all your connected numbers live. Click **Connect Channel** in the top right.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose WhatsApp">
    You'll be asked which platform you want to connect. Pick **WhatsApp**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Stay on Embedded Signup and log in with Facebook">
    A Facebook window opens. Log in, choose your WhatsApp Business Account and the phone number you want to use, then let the window close on its own.

    You'll also see a **Manual** tab next to it. it's for businesses that already connected to WhatsApp through Meta directly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm your number is Active">
    Back on the Channels page, your number now shows **Active** status — it's ready to send and receive messages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Full walkthrough: [Connecting WhatsApp](/core-setup/channels/whatsapp).

## Step 3: Get a template approved

Do this before adding contacts. Once your template is with Meta, the review runs in the background and you can carry on with Step 4 while it runs.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Templates → WhatsApp">
    In the sidebar, open **Templates**, then **WhatsApp**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Already have templates approved in Meta?">
    Click **Sync Templates**. WhatsMark pulls in everything already approved on your WhatsApp Business Account, and you can skip straight to Step 4 — no waiting at all.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Otherwise, click Create Template">
    Write your message, then submit it to Meta for review.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Watch for the status to change">
    Your template shows **PENDING** while Meta reviews it, then **APPROVED** once it's cleared.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  You can only send a template that shows **APPROVED**. If Meta rejects it, you'll see the reason on the Templates page and can edit and resubmit.
</Warning>

More detail: [WhatsApp Templates](/messaging/whatsapp-templates).

## Step 4: Add a contact

You only need one person to test with use your own number if you like.

Go to **Contacts** and click **Create Contact**. If you already have a customer list ready, click **Import Contacts** instead and upload a CSV or spreadsheet.

See [Managing Contacts](/contacts/managing-contacts) and [Importing & Exporting](/contacts/import-export).

## Step 5: Send the template

Your number is connected, your template is approved, and you have someone to message. Now you send that approved template to them — remember, it has to be a template, because this is your first contact with that person.

### To one person

Find the contact in your **Contacts** list, open the row's action menu (the three dots on the right), and choose **Initiate chat**. Pick your approved template and send.

This is the quickest way to confirm everything works end to end.

### To many people at once

Go to **Campaigns** and click **Create Campaign**. On the first step, choose **Template Campaign** — it broadcasts your approved template to a whole group of contacts.

<Note>
  You'll also see a **Message Campaign** option, which sends free-form messages in your own wording. It only reaches people who messaged you in the last 24 hours, so it can't be your *first* message to a new contact. Start with a Template Campaign.
</Note>

See [Campaigns](/messaging/campaigns).

## Step 6: Reply when they answer

Once your message lands and someone replies, the conversation appears in **Chat** — your shared team box.

Remember, their reply opens that 24-hour window, so you can now answer in your own words without a template.

If other people will be handling replies too, [invite your team](/core-setup/team-and-roles) so everyone works from the same inbox.
