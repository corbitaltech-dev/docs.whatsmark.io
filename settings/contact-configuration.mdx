When someone messages your WhatsApp number for the first time and no matching contact exists, WhatsMark.io creates one automatically. Configure the defaults applied to that new contact under **Settings → Contacts**.

## Global Defaults

Applied to every channel unless a channel-level override is configured.

- **Lifecycle** — the contact type: Guest, Lead, or Customer.
- **Status** — required. Falls back to your default status if left unset.
- **Source** — required. Falls back to a "WhatsApp" source if left unset.
- **Assign To** — required. Controls who gets assigned to the auto-created contact:
  - **Unassigned** — the contact isn't assigned to anyone.
  - **Round Robin** — cycles through every team member, in order, one assignment at a time.
  - **Online Team Member** — cycles through whichever team members are currently online; if nobody is online, it falls back to Round Robin across everyone.
  - **Custom Team Member** — always assigns to one specific person, picked from the **Team Member** field that appears once you select this option.
- **WhatsApp Channel** — required. The channel these defaults apply to when no channel-specific override exists.
- **Labels** and **Groups** — optional, applied to auto-created contacts.

## Per-channel overrides

If you have multiple WhatsApp numbers connected, expand a channel below the global defaults and turn on its **Override** switch to set different Lifecycle, Status, Source, Assign To, Labels, and Groups values just for that channel.

<Tip>
Turning a channel's override off falls that channel back to the global defaults automatically — its own saved values are kept but ignored until you turn it on again.
</Tip>
