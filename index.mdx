## Before you start

Two things to have ready:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A phone number for WhatsApp" icon="smartphone">
    It needs to be **either**:

    - a number that isn't on WhatsApp at all, **or**
    - a number you're already using in the **WhatsApp Business app**.

    <Tip>
      Already running your business on the WhatsApp Business app? You **don't** have to give it up. WhatsMark.io can connect alongside it — you keep using the app on your phone, your old chats come across, and new messages appear in both places.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A Facebook account with a business profile on Meta" icon="facebook">
    WhatsApp belongs to Meta, so connecting a business number goes through them. A **Meta Business account** is enough to get started. Getting your business verified by Meta isn't required on day one, but it lifts how many people you're allowed to message, so it's worth doing soon.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Set up in 5 steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your free workspace" icon="user-plus">
    Pick a plan on the [pricing page](https://whatsmark.io/pricing) and click **Get Started**. Fill in your name, email, password, and WhatsApp number, then choose a name for your workspace — you get your own web address, like `acme.whatsmark.io`.

    <Note>
      Free Forever workspaces open straight away. If you chose a paid plan, you'll be asked to pay first. See [Free vs. paid plans](/getting-started/free-vs-paid).
    </Note>

    Details: [Register](/getting-started/register)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your WhatsApp number" icon="message-circle">
    Nothing can be sent or received until a number is connected. Go to **Channels → Connect Channel**, choose **WhatsApp**, and use **Embedded Signup** — Meta's own pop-up window walks you through logging in and picking your number.

    When the pop-up closes, your number should show as **Active**. That's it.

    <Tip>
      You can connect more than one number, and a Facebook Messenger page too, and handle them all from the same inbox.
    </Tip>

    Details: [Connecting WhatsApp](/core-setup/channels/whatsapp)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get one message approved" icon="file-text">
    Before you can send the first message to someone, WhatsApp requires you to use an **approved message template**. This applies to every business on WhatsApp, not just WhatsMark.io.

    A **message template** is a reusable message that you can personalize with details like the customer's name, order number, or appointment time.

    - **Starting fresh?** Go to **Templates → WhatsApp → Create Template**, write your message, and submit it. Approval usually takes a few minutes.
    - **Already using another WhatsApp provider?** Click **Sync Templates** and every message you've already had approved is pulled in instantly — no waiting at all.

    <Warning>
      A template has to show **APPROVED** before you can send it. This is the step most likely to keep you waiting, so start it early — the review runs in the background while you carry on with the next step.
    </Warning>

    Details: [WhatsApp templates](/messaging/whatsapp-templates)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the people you want to message" icon="contact">
    Go to **Contacts** and click **Create Contact**. One person is enough to test with — use your own number if you like. If you already have a customer list, click **Import Contacts** instead and upload a CSV or spreadsheet.

    Details: [Managing contacts](/contacts/managing-contacts) · [Importing a list](/contacts/import-export)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send your first message" icon="send">
    Two easy ways to start:

    - **To one person** — find them in your **Contacts** list, open the row's action menu (the three dots on the right), and choose **Initiate chat**. Pick your approved template and send.
    - **To everyone** — go to **Campaigns → Create Campaign** and choose **Template Campaign**, which broadcasts your approved template to a whole group of contacts.

    Then watch the delivered and read counts come in.

    <Note>
      You'll also see a **Message Campaign** option, which sends free-form messages in your own wording. It only reaches people who messaged you in the last 24 hours, so it can't be your *first* message to a new contact. Start with a Template Campaign.
    </Note>

    Details: [Campaigns](/messaging/campaigns)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Check>
  **Done.** Connect a number → get one message approved → add contacts → send. Everything else in WhatsMark.io builds on top of those four things.
</Check>

## When people reply

Replies land in **Chat** — your shared inbox. Anyone on your team can pick up a conversation, assign it to a colleague, leave a private note, or send a saved reply.

One thing worth knowing: once a customer messages you, you can write back **freely** for 24 hours — any wording you like. After 24 hours of silence from them, you can only reach out again using an approved template. That single rule explains most of "why can't I send this?" moments. ## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does it really cost nothing to try?">
    Yes. The **Free Forever** plan needs no credit card — you get connected numbers, contacts, chatbot flows and a monthly allowance of AI usage without paying anything. When you outgrow it, the paid plan raises every limit. See [Free vs. paid plans](/getting-started/free-vs-paid).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will I lose my existing WhatsApp number or my old chats?">
    No. If your number is on the **WhatsApp Business app**, WhatsMark.io can connect alongside it — you keep the app on your phone, your past conversations are brought in, and new messages show up in both places.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why can't I just type whatever I want to a new customer?">
    WhatsApp only lets businesses open a conversation with wording it has approved in advance — that's what a template is. Once the customer replies, you can write freely for the next 24 hours. [More on the rules](/getting-started/concepts).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long does approval take?">
    Usually a few minutes. Occasionally longer. You'll get a notification the moment the status changes, and you can carry on setting everything else up while you wait.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I'm already with another WhatsApp provider. Can I move across?">
    Yes, and you don't start from zero. Connect the same number, then click **Sync Templates** on the Templates page to pull in every message you've already had approved. Your contacts come across from a spreadsheet.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I connect WhatsMark.io to my own software?">
    Yes, if you want to. There's a [developer API](/overview) for sending messages and managing contacts from your own systems. Nothing in this guide needs it — everything above is done from the screen.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
