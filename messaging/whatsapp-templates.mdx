WhatsApp requires any message sent outside the 24-hour customer service window — including every broadcast — to use a pre-approved **template**. WhatsMark's Templates page is where you build, submit, and track them.

## Browsing templates

The Templates table lists every template across your connected accounts, filterable by account, status, category, and language, with a quality-rating badge on each.

## Creating a template

<Steps>
<Step title="Pick a type">
  Choose **Header**, **Carousel**, or **Authentication**.
</Step>

<Step title="Fill in Basic Info">
  Select channel, Name (lowercase letters, numbers, and underscores only), language, and category — these become locked once the template is submitted.
</Step>

<Step title="Build the Header">
  Text (up to 60 characters), or an image, video, or PDF document you upload.
</Step>

<Step title="Write the Body">
  Up to 1,024 characters. Variables are detected live as you type `{{1}}`, `{{2}}`, and so on.
</Step>

<Step title="Add a Footer (optional)">
  A short line of fixed text under the body, up to 60 characters.
</Step>

<Step title="Add Buttons">
  Up to 10 buttons total, each with button text up to 25 characters. Choose from **Quick Reply**, **URL**, **Phone Number**, or **Flow** (opens one of your [WhatsApp Flows](/automation/whatsapp-flows) directly from the message).
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Header media uploads (image, video, or document) accept **JPG, PNG, GIF, WEBP, MP4, or PDF**, up to **16 MB**.
</Note>

For **Carousel** templates, you build 2–10 cards instead, each with an image or video header, body text (up to 160 characters), and 1–2 buttons (Quick Reply or URL). Every card in a carousel must use the same number of buttons with matching types at each position — WhatsMark validates this before submission and tells you exactly which card is inconsistent if not.

For **Authentication** templates, you configure:

- The **OTP button type** — Copy Code, One-Tap, or Zero-Tap.
- **Code expiration** — 1 to 90 minutes.
- **Message send TTL** — 10 to 600 seconds, how long WhatsApp will keep trying to deliver the code.
- An optional **security recommendation** line and **autofill button text** (up to 25 characters).
- For One-Tap and Zero-Tap specifically, your Android app's **package name** and **signature hash** are required so the code can be autofilled, plus accepting Zero-Tap's terms.

A live, WhatsApp-styled preview updates as you build, so you can see exactly what your contact will see.

<Tip>
Don't start from scratch — browse the **Template Library** for pre-built starter templates, tabbed by type and favouritable, and use one to pre-fill the form.
</Tip>

## Submitting for approval

Once you're happy with a template, submit it to Meta for review. Status updates — **Approved**, **Rejected**, **Paused**, or **Disabled** — arrive automatically, along with an in-app notification so you don't have to keep checking back.

<Warning>
Once a template is submitted, its **name**, **language**, and **category** can no longer be changed. You can still edit the content and resubmit, or delete it and start over.
</Warning>

## Managing existing templates

- **Edit** content and resubmit.
- **Delete** a template.
- **Sync** — pull the latest status/content from Meta for a single channel or all channels at once, useful if a template was changed directly in Meta Business Manager.
- **Analytics** — open a template's stats panel to see sent, delivered, read, clicked, and cost figures over a chosen date range.
