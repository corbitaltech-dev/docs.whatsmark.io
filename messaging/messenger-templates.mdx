Unlike WhatsApp, Messenger doesn't require Meta approval for reusable messages. **Messenger Templates** are local presets you create for your own convenience, ready to drop into a conversation or campaign.

## Template types

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Text">
  Plain text with no special formatting.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Quick Replies">
  A message with up to 13 tappable reply chips underneath it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Button Template">
  A message with up to 3 buttons — each a web link, or a postback action.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Generic / Carousel Template">
  Up to 10 cards, each with an image, title, subtitle, and a button.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Media">
  An image, video, audio, or file, referenced by a public HTTPS URL, with an optional caption.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Creating and managing templates

Create a template by choosing one of the types above and filling in its fields. Edit or delete a template at any time — changes only affect future sends, not messages already delivered.

## Where you can use them

Once created, a Messenger template is available to pick from:

- The [Live Chat](/messaging/live-chat) composer.
- The [Initiate Chat](/contacts/managing-contacts#row-actions) flow, for messaging a contact directly.
- [Campaigns](/messaging/campaigns), as the content for a Messenger broadcast.
