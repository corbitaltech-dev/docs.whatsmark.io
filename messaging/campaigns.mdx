**Campaigns** let you send a message to many contacts at once, instead of one conversation at a time. WhatsMark supports two campaign types, each suited to a different situation.

## Campaign types

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Message Campaigns">
  Free-form text sent to contacts who have messaged you within the last 24 hours (the same [24-hour window](/getting-started/concepts#two-whatsapp-rules-that-shape-everything) rule that applies in [Live Chat](/messaging/live-chat)). No template approval needed, but the audience is limited to recently active contacts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Template Campaigns">
  Uses a Meta-approved [WhatsApp template](/messaging/whatsapp-templates), so you can broadcast to any contact list regardless of when they last messaged you.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  Before you can create a campaign you need an **Active channel**, and a **Template Campaign** additionally needs at least one **Approved** template. If you don't have one yet, create or sync one first — see [WhatsApp Templates](/messaging/whatsapp-templates).
</Note>

<Warning>
Each campaign type has its own monthly send quota, tracked independently — sending a lot of Message Campaigns doesn't affect your Template Campaign quota, or vice versa.
</Warning>

## Creating a campaign

Campaigns are built with a 4-step wizard:

<Steps>
<Step title="Basics">
  Name your campaign, choose its type (Message or Template), pick a **channel type** — WhatsApp or Messenger — and select the channel to send from. If you choose a Template Campaign, also select the template you want to send.

  <BrowserFrame
    src="/messaging/img/campaign/campaign-basics.png"
    url="whatsmark.io/campaigns/create"
    alt="Campaign basics step with a campaign name field, a choice between Template Campaign and Message Campaign, a WhatsApp or Messenger channel type toggle, and channel and template pickers"
    caption="Image shows the campaign basics step of the wizard."
  />
</Step>

<Step title="Audience">
  Choose who receives it — a contact **group**, a **filtered** segment, or a **manual** selection of specific contacts.

  <BrowserFrame
    src="/messaging/img/campaign/campaign-audience.png"
    url="whatsmark.io/campaigns/create"
    alt="Audience step with filters for status, source, group, and type above a searchable contact list with checkboxes and a Select all filtered option"
    caption="Image shows the audience step of the wizard."
  />
</Step>

<Step title="Content">
  Compose your message or select a template — header, body, footer, buttons, or carousel cards as applicable — upload any media, and insert **merge fields** (see below) to personalize each message.

  <BrowserFrame
    src="/messaging/img/campaign/campaign-content.png"
    url="whatsmark.io/campaigns/create"
    alt="Content step for a template campaign showing a header image upload area and a field for filling in the template's body variable"
    caption="Image shows the content step of the wizard."
  />
</Step>

<Step title="Schedule">
  Send immediately, or schedule it for a specific date and time.

  <BrowserFrame
    src="/messaging/img/campaign/campaign-schedule.png"
    url="whatsmark.io/campaigns/create"
    alt="Schedule step with a choice between Send now and Schedule for later, with a date and time picker open beside a summary of the campaign"
    caption="Image shows the schedule step of the wizard."
  />
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Eligible marketing sends are automatically routed through Meta's **Marketing Messages Lite** endpoint when your connected WABA is enrolled — this happens silently, with an automatic fallback to a standard send if it's not available. You don't need to configure anything.
</Info>

## Managing a running campaign

<BrowserFrame
  src="/messaging/img/campaign/campaign-detail.png"
  url="whatsmark.io/campaigns"
  alt="Campaign detail page with a Completed status, cards for total sent, delivered, not received, read, and failed, an engagement breakdown chart covering delivered, read, replied, clicks, and failed, and a preview of the message as recipients see it"
  caption="Image shows the campaign detail page tracking delivery and engagement."
/>

From a campaign's detail page you can:

- **Launch**, **pause**, **resume**, or **cancel** it.
- **Resend to failed recipients only** — retry just the sends that didn't go through.
- **Clone for replied** — create a new campaign targeting only the contacts who replied to this one.
- View **delivery analytics** — sent, delivered, not received, read, and failed counts, plus an engagement breakdown covering replies and clicks.
- Preview your message exactly as recipients see it.
- Browse the paginated **recipients** table for that campaign.

<Tip>
Use "Clone for replied" to build a simple follow-up sequence — send an initial broadcast, then automatically target only the people who engaged with it.
</Tip>

## Merge fields

While composing a campaign, use the `@` picker to insert **merge fields** — tokens like the contact's name or a custom field value that get replaced with real data for each recipient at send time. See the full list on the [Merge Fields](/messaging/merge-fields) reference page.
