**Chat** is where your team actually talks to customers — a unified inbox for every WhatsApp and Messenger conversation across all your connected channels.

<Note>
  On a brand-new workspace, or before you've connected a channel, Chat just shows a friendly empty state rather than an explicit "connect a channel" prompt — head to [Channels](/getting-started/overview#connect-a-whatsapp-business-account) first if you don't see any conversations here.
</Note>

## The inbox layout

Chat is split into four panes:

1. **Sidebar** — filters: **All**, **Mine**, **Unassigned**, a **Not Replied** toggle, per-**contact-status**, per-**Team Inbox**, and a **date** filter, each with a live counter.
2. **Conversation list** — every conversation matching the current filter, searchable by contact, sortable **Newest** / **Oldest** first, and filterable by channel from the list header.
3. **Message thread** — the open conversation, with message search within the thread.
4. **Contact panel** — the contact's details, media gallery, and notes, without leaving the chat.

The **sidebar** filters your inbox, and the **conversation list** shows everything matching the current filter.

![The Chat sidebar with All, Mine, Unassigned, Status, and Team Inbox filters, next to the conversation list showing contact rows with status labels, avatars, and last-message previews](/messaging/img/chat/chat-inbox-layout.png)

The contact panel isn't read-only — you can **edit the contact's details directly from here** (name, status, labels, and other fields), the same as editing them from the [Contacts](/contacts/managing-contacts) page, and add or review [notes](/contacts/managing-contacts#notes) without switching pages.

![The contact panel on the right of Chat showing Contact details with fields for Name, Phone Number, Email Address, Source, Contact Type, Assigned to, Labels, and Channel, plus an Edit button](/messaging/img/chat/chat-contact-panel-details.png)

Updates appear instantly — new messages, delivery ticks, assignment changes, and counters all update live without needing to refresh the page.

## Starting a new chat

You don't have to wait for a customer to message first — click **New Chat** to search your existing contacts and open a conversation directly from Chat:

- If the contact already has an open conversation (within the 24-hour window), it opens immediately.
- If their window is closed, or they're reachable on more than one platform, you'll be guided through a **channel picker** and then the [Initiate Chat](/contacts/managing-contacts#row-actions) template flow instead.

![The New chat sheet with a search box to find a contact by name, phone, or email, and a list of contacts each showing Open or Template status](/messaging/img/chat/chat-new-chat.png)

## Browsing message history

Use the **Filter by date** button in the sidebar to open a **date range picker** and jump to an earlier point in your history. Picking a range both narrows the conversation list to that window and reloads the open conversation's messages for it; clear the date chip to return to the live view.

<Note>
How far back you can go is limited by your plan's chat history retention. Dates outside your plan's window are disabled in the picker, with a link to [Billing](/billing/plans-and-subscription) if you need to look further back.
</Note>

## Sending a message

The composer sits at the bottom of a conversation. Text formatting (bold, italic, strikethrough, and lists) is available above it, and each numbered icon below opens a specific way to send something.

<BrowserFrame
  src="/messaging/img/chat/chat-composer-annotated.png"
  url="whatsmark.io/chat"
  alt="Chat composer with each toolbar icon numbered 1 through 9"
  caption="Image shows the composer with each toolbar icon numbered."
/>

1. **Emoji** — insert an emoji into your message.
2. **Attach image/file** — upload an image, video, audio, or document, with upload progress shown as it sends.
3. **Location** — send a location pin.
4. **Contact card** — share one of your existing contacts, or fill in someone else's details and send them as a card. Details you enter manually this way are only sent in the message and aren't added to your contact list.
5. **Canned reply** — open the searchable picker of pre-written responses. You can also open it by typing **`/`** directly in the composer.
6. **Run a flow** — start a bot automation flow directly from the conversation.
7. **Send form** — send a WhatsApp Flow, such as an appointment booking form (WhatsApp channels only).
8. **AI assist** — improve, fix grammar, make formal or casual, shorten, translate, or apply one of your **Custom AI Prompts** to your drafted message. Gated by your plan — see [AI Features](/automation/ai-features) for the full picture.
9. **Send template** — send an approved template for this contact's channel.

<Warning>
WhatsApp only allows free-form messages within a **[24-hour window](/getting-started/concepts#two-whatsapp-rules-that-shape-everything)** after the customer's last message. A live countdown above the composer shows exactly how much time is left. Once that window closes, the composer shows a **"Send template"** prompt instead — see [Initiate Chat](/contacts/managing-contacts#row-actions) for sending an approved template to restart the conversation. Messenger conversations don't have this restriction, so no countdown appears for them.
</Warning>

## Managing a conversation

- **Assign** a conversation to one or more team members, or to a **Team Inbox**.
- **Clear** the message history or **delete** the conversation entirely. (Opening a conversation marks it as read automatically — its unread counter clears the moment you open it.)
- **Delete a single message** — you (or a teammate) can **restore** it within a 2-minute window.
- **Run an automation flow** manually against the open conversation, or send a **WhatsApp Flow** interactively — see [Automation Flows](/automation/chatbot-flows) and [WhatsApp Flows](/automation/whatsapp-flows).

<Tip>
**Assigning** conversations (to a person or a Team Inbox) is limited to workspace **owners and admins**. Other actions — running flows, sending templates, clearing, and deleting — respect your workspace's [roles and permissions](/core-setup/team-and-roles), so a team member only sees the actions their role allows.
</Tip>

<Note>
**Who sees which conversations:** owners and admins see every conversation. Any other team member only sees conversations **assigned to them** or belonging to a **Team Inbox they're a member of** — so assignment is also how you route work to non-admin staff.
</Note>

## Team Inboxes

Beyond assigning conversations to individuals, you can group team members into **Team Inboxes** (e.g. "Sales", "Support"):

<Steps>
<Step title="Create a Team Inbox">
  Give it a name from the Team sheet.

  ![The Create Team Inbox sheet with a Team Name field (placeholder e.g. Sales, Support, Billing), a Team Members multi-select, and Cancel and Create Team buttons](/messaging/img/chat/chat-create-team-inbox.png)
</Step>

<Step title="Add members">
  Add the team members who should see conversations routed to this inbox.
</Step>

<Step title="Assign conversations to it">
  From any conversation, assign it to the Team Inbox instead of (or alongside) an individual — everyone in that inbox sees it in their sidebar.
</Step>
</Steps>
