WhatsMark sits on top of the official **WhatsApp Business Platform**, which has a few rules that shape everything you do in the product. Understanding these first will save you a lot of "why can't I send this?" moments later.

<Info>
  New here? Read this page once, then jump to the [Quickstart](/quickstart) to send your first message.
</Info>

## Workspace, channel, and WABA

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Workspace (tenant)">
    Your isolated account on WhatsMark — its own subdomain (e.g. `acme.whatsmark.io`), database, users, contacts, and billing. Everything you do happens inside your workspace.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Channel">
    A connected messaging account. Most commonly a **WhatsApp Business Account (WABA)** and phone number, connected through Meta's Embedded Signup or manually. A workspace can connect several channels, including Facebook **Messenger** Pages. Nothing sends or receives until you connect at least one channel.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="WABA & phone number">
    A WhatsApp Business Account is your business identity on Meta's platform; each WABA has one or more phone numbers. Your **quality rating** and **messaging limits** (below) are tracked per phone number.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The 24-hour customer service window

When a customer messages you, a **24-hour window** opens for that conversation. Inside the window you can reply with **free-form (session) messages** — plain text, media, whatever you like. Once 24 hours pass with no new inbound message from the customer, the window closes.

<Warning>
  Outside the 24-hour window you **cannot** send a free-form message. You can only re-open the conversation with an approved **message template** (below). This is a WhatsApp platform rule, not a WhatsMark limitation.
</Warning>

This is why the window matters throughout WhatsMark:

- In [Live Chat](/messaging/live-chat), the composer lets you type freely while the window is open; once it closes, you'll be prompted to send a template instead.
- A **message campaign** (free-form broadcast) can only target contacts who currently have an open window — so it can't be your first outreach to cold contacts.

## Template messages vs. session messages

| | Session message | Template message |
|---|---|---|
| **When** | Only inside an open 24-hour window | Any time, including the first message to a contact |
| **Content** | Free-form (text, media, etc.) | Pre-approved template, with variables filled in |
| **Approval** | None | Must be **approved by Meta** before use |

Because a template is the *only* way to start a conversation or reach someone outside the window, **getting a template approved is the single most important setup step** before you can broadcast or cold-message anyone.

## Template categories

Every template belongs to a category, which Meta uses to price and rate-limit it:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Marketing">
    Promotions, offers, announcements, re-engagement. Subject to per-user marketing limits and the recipient's marketing opt-out.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Utility">
    Transactional updates tied to an existing order or account — order confirmations, shipping updates, appointment reminders.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Authentication">
    One-time passcodes and verification codes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

See [WhatsApp Templates](/messaging/whatsapp-templates) to create one, or sync templates you've already had approved in Meta.

## Approval & quality rating

- **Approval** — a new template is submitted to Meta and stays in a pending state until it's **approved** or **rejected** (usually minutes, sometimes longer). You can't send a template until it's approved. WhatsMark notifies you when the status changes.
- **Quality rating** — Meta scores each phone number (Green / Yellow / Red) based on how recipients react (blocks, reports, reads). A low rating can lower your messaging limits. You can see channel health on the [Channels](/core-setup/channels/whatsapp#waba-health) page.

## Messaging limits & opt-in

- **Messaging limits (tiers)** — WhatsApp caps how many unique customers you can *start* conversations with in a rolling 24 hours (e.g. 250 → 1K → 10K → unlimited). Good quality and volume automatically raise your tier over time.
- **Opt-in** — you must have permission to message people. Sending to contacts who didn't opt in leads to blocks and reports, which hurt your quality rating and can get your number flagged.

<Note>
  Separately from WhatsApp's own limits, your **WhatsMark plan** sets quotas (contacts, campaigns, AI tokens, storage, channels). See [Dashboard & Feature Usage](/reporting/dashboard-and-usage) to track them and [Plans & Subscription](/billing/plans-and-subscription) to raise them.
</Note>
