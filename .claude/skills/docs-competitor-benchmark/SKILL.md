# Benchmarking the docs against competitors

Use this before writing any page whose job is to **win a customer**, not just instruct one: the quick start, the docs home, feature overviews, migration pages.

The goal is never to copy their copy. It is to find (a) the structural patterns that reduce drop-off, and (b) the things we can say truthfully that they can't.

---

## The five to check

| Competitor | Docs | Watch for |
|---|---|---|
| **AiSensy** | `wiki.aisensy.com` | Leads hard on free access. Has a dedicated *migrate from your existing provider* article — a deliberate competitor-poaching play. |
| **Wati** | `support.wati.io`, `docs.wati.io` | Prerequisites-first. Tells you the phone-number and Business-Manager requirements before anything else. |
| **respond.io** | `respond.io/help/quick-start` | Onboarding as an explicit checklist module: connect channel → organise contacts → AI → invite teammates. |
| **BotSailor** | `botsailor.com` docs | Video-led, feature-dump. A model of what *not* to do — long, jargon-heavy, no narrative. |
| **Interakt** | `interakt.shop` | Strong "WhatsApp API onboarding checklist" SEO content. |

## Method

1. **Search, don't guess.** `WebSearch` for `<competitor> docs getting started onboarding steps`, then `WebFetch` the actual collection or quick-start page. Their structure is the data; secondhand summaries are not.
2. **Record the step order, not the prose.** What do they make you do first? Where does the free-plan pitch appear? Where does migration appear?
3. **Find the drop-off guards.** Prerequisites sections, "before you begin" warnings, time estimates. These exist because their support team got tired of the same ticket — free research.
4. **Find what they avoid saying.** If nobody mentions keeping your existing number, that's a gap we can own.
5. **Cross-check every claimed advantage against the product** before it goes in a page — see the `docs-fact-check` skill. An advantage we don't actually ship is worse than no advantage.

## Patterns already adopted (don't re-derive these)

- **Prerequisites first** (from Wati) — the "Before you start" section. Highest-value block on the quick start; it kills the most common support ticket.
- **Checklist framing** (from respond.io) — numbered `<Steps>`, a `<Check>` recap, then a "Keep going" card group.
- **Free access up front** (from AiSensy) — the Free Forever card sits above the fold, before the feature list.
- **A migration answer** (from AiSensy) — "I'm already with another provider, can I move across?" in the FAQ, answered with **Sync Templates**.
- **Rejected:** BotSailor-style feature dumps and jargon. Outcomes, not module names.

## Our honest differentiators

Verified against the product. Lead with these — competitors either can't offer them or don't advertise them.

1. **Coexistence** — you keep using the WhatsApp Business app on your phone, your old chats come across, and messages stay in sync. Most competitors require you to surrender the number to the API. This is the strongest objection-killer we have; put it in prerequisites *and* the FAQ.
2. **Instant template sync** — one click pulls in every template already approved on your WhatsApp Business account, so a migrating customer skips the wait entirely.
3. **AI flow generation** — describe a chatbot in plain English and it gets built. Available on the Free Forever plan, which is unusual.
4. **Free Forever includes the AI assistant** — most competitors gate AI behind a paid tier.

**Rules when using these:** never name a competitor on a docs page — describe the benefit, not the comparison. Never state a competitor's pricing or limits; they change and being wrong is worse than being silent. Re-verify a differentiator before reusing it — coexistence availability and plan gating have both moved before.
