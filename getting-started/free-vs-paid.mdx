Both plans are the same product. You get the shared inbox, campaigns, chatbot flows and the AI
assistant either way. The plan decides **how much** you can use and **which** extras are unlocked.

<Info>
  For current prices, see the [pricing page](https://whatsmark.io/pricing). We keep prices off this
  page so you never read an out-of-date figure here.
</Info>

## Which one is right for you

<CardGroup cols={2}>

### How much of each

Everything else is a question of *how much*, and Professional gives you more of all of it —
contacts, connected numbers, broadcasts each month, chatbot flows, AI usage, booking forms,
media storage, and how far back your chat history goes.

<Info>
  **We don't list those numbers here, because they change.** You'll always find the current
  figures in two places that can't go stale:

  - The [pricing page](https://whatsmark.io/pricing), before you sign up.
  - **Feature Usage** inside your workspace, which shows every allowance alongside how much of
    it you've actually used.
</Info>

<Note>
  Two words worth knowing when you look at those figures. A **limit** is a ceiling on what you
  hold right now — delete something and the slot frees up. A **monthly allowance** is a counter
  that resets on the 1st of each calendar month, whatever date you subscribed, and deleting
  something doesn't refund it.

  Broadcast allowances count **campaigns created**, not messages sent — one campaign to a
  thousand people still counts as one.
</Note>

<Tip>
  On Professional, some allowances can be raised on their own without changing plan — contacts,
  team seats and AI usage. See [Add-ons & upgrades](/billing/addons-and-upgrades).
</Tip>

## Hitting a limit never deletes anything

Worth saying plainly, because it's the thing people worry about most: when you reach a limit,
you're stopped from adding more and told which limit you've reached. **Nothing you already have
is removed, hidden, or switched off.** That's true on Free, and it's true if you downgrade later.

## When it's time to move up

You don't have to guess. These are the moments people actually move:

- **Someone else needs to answer messages.** Free is a one-person workspace. The moment a
  colleague needs their own login, you've outgrown it — this is the clearest signal of all.
- **You keep running out of broadcasts before the month is up.** Note *keep* — see below.
- **Your contact list outgrew the Free allowance.** Usually the first wall anyone hits — imports
  stop once you're at the ceiling.
- **The AI assistant stops partway through the month.** Fine for occasional rewrites; a team
  using it daily will finish the allowance early.
- **You're pasting the same reply for the tenth time today.** Quick replies are Professional.
- **You want your shop or your own software connected.** Shopify, WooCommerce, Google Sheets,
  Calendar, webhooks and the API are all Professional, with no partial version on Free.

### Free is still right for you if…

- You're the only person answering messages.
- Your customer list is small and growing slowly.
- You send the occasional campaign rather than a monthly programme.
- You don't need your shop or other tools wired in yet.

<Tip>
  **One busy month isn't a reason to upgrade.** If you ran out of campaigns once because of a
  seasonal push, the allowance comes back on the 1st. Upgrade when you're hitting the ceiling
  *repeatedly*, not the first time it happens.
</Tip>

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I have to upgrade at some point?">
    No. Free Forever is a plan, not a trial. Stay on it as long as it fits how you work.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need a credit card to start?">
    No. Free Forever workspaces open straight away.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens when I hit a limit?">
    You're stopped from adding more and told which limit you've reached. Delete a contact and you
    can add another straight away — but a monthly allowance only comes back on the 1st, so
    deleting a campaign doesn't refund one.

    Your **Dashboard** shows usage for the headline limits, and **Feature Usage** lists every one
    with what you've used and what's left. See
    [Dashboard & usage](/reporting/dashboard-and-usage).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does upgrading affect my data?">
    No. Contacts, chats, templates and flows all carry over untouched, and the higher limits
    apply as soon as payment goes through.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data if I downgrade?">
    Nothing is deleted. You keep everything you have — you simply can't add more in a category
    where you're now over the cap until you're back under it. Your contacts, conversations and
    templates stay exactly where they are.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I raise just one limit instead of upgrading?">
    On Professional, yes — contacts, team seats and AI allowance can each be topped up on their
    own. See [Add-ons & upgrades](/billing/addons-and-upgrades).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does WhatsApp charge me separately?">
    Yes — Meta charges for some messages regardless of which plan you're on, and that's separate
    from what you pay us.
    [How WhatsApp charges you](/getting-started/concepts#what-whatsapp-charges-you) explains
    what's free and what isn't.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about agencies reselling to clients?">
    There's a **Reseller** plan for that. It isn't self-serve yet — you can join the waitlist
    from the [pricing page](https://whatsmark.io/pricing).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
