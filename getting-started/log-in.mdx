Every workspace has its own web address, like `acme.whatsmark.io`, and you sign in there. If you
remember your address, go straight to it. If you don't, we'll find it for you from your email.

## Don't know your workspace address?

Go to the main [whatsmark.io](https://whatsmark.io) site and choose **Log in**. You'll land on a
**"Find your workspace"** page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Type the email you signed up with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue">
    We look up which workspace that email belongs to and send you to its login page, with your
    email already filled in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If no account matches, you'll see a neutral message rather than "no account found". That's on
  purpose — it stops anyone using this page to check whether an email is registered.
</Note>

## Signing in

<BrowserFrame
  src="/images/login.png"

  caption="Your workspace's login page."
/>

On your workspace's login page (**"Welcome back"**), enter your **email** and **password**, then
click **Log in**. The **Forgot password?** link sits right next to the password box — see below
if you need it.

<Tip>
  You can also sign in with **Google** or **Facebook** — look for the buttons under
  **"Or continue with"**. This works as long as that Google or Facebook account uses the same
  email as your workspace.
</Tip>

<Note>
  For security, sign-in is rate-limited: after several failed tries for the same email in a
  minute, you'll be asked to wait a short while before trying again. It clears on its own.
</Note>

## Forgot your password

No problem — you can reset it yourself.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Forgot your password?'">
    It's next to the password box on the login page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    We'll email a reset link to that address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the link and set a new one">
    The link opens a **"Choose a new password"** page. Your new password follows the same rules as
    sign-up — at least 8 characters, with an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, a number and a
    symbol.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If the email doesn't arrive, check your spam or junk folder. Reset links expire after a while,
  so if yours has, just request a new one.
</Note>

## Add two-factor security (2FA)

Want to make your account more secure? After you log in, go to your **Profile** and turn on
two-factor security. It asks for a short code from your phone on top of your password, so your
account stays safe even if someone learns the password.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn it on">
    Open the **Two-Factor Authentication** card in your profile and click **Enable**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the code">
    Scan the QR code with an authenticator app — Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, or any
    similar one — then enter the 6-digit code it shows to confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your recovery codes">
    You'll get a set of one-time **recovery codes**. Keep them somewhere safe — any one of them
    logs you in if you ever lose your phone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After this, every login asks for the **6-digit code** from your app once you've entered your
password. Lost your phone? Choose **login using a recovery code** and use one of the codes you
saved.
