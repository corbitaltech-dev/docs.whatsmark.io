Because every WhatsMark.io customer has their own workspace subdomain (e.g. `acme.whatsmark.io`), logging in has two possible starting points depending on where you begin.

## Finding your workspace

If you go to the main WhatsMark.io site (rather than your own subdomain) and select **Log in**, you'll see a **"Find your workspace"** page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Type the email address you registered with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue">
    Click **Continue**. WhatsMark.io looks up which workspace that email belongs to and redirects you to that workspace's own login page, with your email pre-filled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  For privacy, if no account matches the email you entered, you'll see a generic message rather than a "no account found" error — this prevents anyone from using this form to check whether an email is registered.
</Note>

If you already know your workspace address, you can skip this step and go straight to `yourworkspace.whatsmark.io/login`.

## Logging in

<BrowserFrame
  src="/images/login.png"
  alt="Workspace login page"
  caption="Login page for your WhatsMark.io workspace"
/>

On your workspace's login page, sign in with:

- **Email address**
- **Password**
- **Remember me** (optional) — stays signed in across browser sessions

If social login is available, you'll also see **"Or continue with"** buttons for Google or Facebook.

<Warning>
  For security, login attempts are rate-limited — after too many failed attempts for the same email and IP address within a minute, you'll need to wait before trying again.
</Warning>

## Two-factor authentication (2FA)

You can turn it on yourself from the **Two-Factor Authentication** card on your **Profile** page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable 2FA">
    Click **Enable** on the Two-Factor Authentication card in your profile settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Scan the QR code with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, etc.) and enter the confirmation code shown by the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your recovery codes">
    WhatsMark.io shows a set of one-time recovery codes — store these somewhere safe. You can use one to log in if you ever lose access to your authenticator app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once enabled, every future login will prompt you for a **6-digit authentication code** after your password, with the option to **"login using a recovery code"** instead if you don't have your device on hand.

## Forgot your password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Forgot your password?'">
    This link sits next to the password field on the login page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Submit your email address on the **Forgot Password** page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox">
    You'll receive a password reset link by email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a new password">
    Clicking the link opens the **Reset Password** page, where you'll set a new password (following the same strength rules as registration: 8\+ characters, with uppercase, lowercase, a number, and a symbol).
  </Step>
</Steps>
