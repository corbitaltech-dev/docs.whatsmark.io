Before you start setting things up, it helps to know a few words that come up again and again in WhatsMark.io. Read this once and the rest of the guides will make sense.

<Info>
  Already know your way around WhatsApp Business? Skip straight to the [Quickstart](/quickstart).
</Info>

## The building blocks

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Workspace — your company's account in WhatsMark.io">
  **Your company's dedicated space inside WhatsMark.io.**

  You get a workspace when you sign up, with your own address, such as `acme.whatsmark.io`. Your team, contacts, conversations, and settings are stored securely within your workspace. Other companies cannot access your data.

Everything you do in WhatsMark.io happens inside your workspace.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Channel — a WhatsApp number you have connected">
  **A WhatsApp Business number connected to WhatsMark.io.**

  This is what sends and receives your messages. Connect more than one if you want — for example, one number for sales and one for support. A Facebook Messenger page can also be a channel.

  Nothing can be sent or received until you connect at least one.

  See [Connecting WhatsApp](/core-setup/channels/whatsapp).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Contact — one customer's details">
  **One person you can message.**

  A contact stores their phone number, and anything else you want to save — name, email, city, or your own [custom fields](/core-setup/custom-fields).

  Add them one by one, or [import your full list](/contacts/import-export) from an Excel or CSV file.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Conversation — one customer's chat">
  **The chat between your business and one customer.**

  It works just like WhatsApp on your phone: every message you both send stays together in one thread. Your team opens and replies to these in [Live Chat](/messaging/live-chat).

  A contact is the person. A conversation is the chat with that person.

  <Note>
    Your bill also uses this word. WhatsApp counts one *new conversation* whenever a chat begins after a quiet gap, and every plan includes a set number each month. See [Free vs. Paid Plans](/getting-started/free-vs-paid).
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Template — a message Meta has approved">
  **A ready-made message that Meta has checked and approved.**

  WhatsApp does not allow you to message a new customer in your own words. You must send an approved template first.

  One template works for every customer, because you can leave blanks that fill in automatically. Write `Hi {{1}}, your order {{2}} is out for delivery` once, and each person gets their own name and order number.

  See [WhatsApp Templates](/messaging/whatsapp-templates) and [Merge fields](/messaging/merge-fields).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Campaign — one message sent to many people">
  **One message sent to many contacts together.**

  For example, a Diwali offer, a new price list, or a payment reminder.

  Choose who receives it — everyone, one group, or a smaller selected list — pick an approved template, then send it now or schedule it for later.

  See [Campaigns](/messaging/campaigns).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Automation — replies that run on their own">
  **Rules that reply to customers for you, day and night.**

  For example, when a customer sends "price", the automation sends your price list and asks which product they need. Nobody has to be online.

  Build it by dragging blocks on screen, or just describe what you want in plain English and let AI create the first version.

  See [Automation Flows](/automation/chatbot-flows).
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Two WhatsApp rules that shape everything

These rules come from WhatsApp, not from WhatsMark. Every tool built on the WhatsApp Business Platform follows them.

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="You need an approved template to message someone first">
  You cannot type a fresh message and send it to a customer who has not first messaged you. You must use a template that Meta has already approved.

  Approval usually takes a few minutes. Once approved, that template can go to anyone, any number of times.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="When a customer replies, you get 24 hours">
  The moment a customer messages you, a **24-hour window** opens. Inside it you can reply normally — type anything, send photos or files, no template message needed.

  Each new message from the customer restarts the 24 hours, so an active chat stays open. The window only closes after a full day of silence from their side.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

| Your situation | What you can send |
| --- | --- |
| Messaging a new customer | An approved template |
| Customer replied within 24 hours | Anything you like, in your own words |
| Customer has been quiet over 24 hours | An approved template |

<Tip>
  This is why your first job in WhatsMark is getting one template approved. Without it, you cannot start a single conversation.
</Tip>
