**Appointments** is where bookings made through an [Appointment Booking flow](/automation/whatsapp-flows#appointment-booking-flows) — or your public booking link — land, and where you configure how booking works.

<Warning>
Appointments are limited by your plan across three dimensions: **active booking forms** (`appointment_forms`), **concurrent active bookings** (`appointments`), and **new bookings per month** (`appointments_monthly`). Free-plan workspaces get 1 form, 1 concurrent booking, and 30 bookings/month; Professional plans get 10 forms, 10 concurrent bookings, and 500/month; Reseller plans are unlimited across all three.
</Warning>

## Viewing appointments

Switch between a **List** view and a **Calendar** (month grid) view, and filter by channel, source, status, or date range. Open any appointment to update its status (for example, marking it completed or cancelled).

## Booking settings

Go to **Settings** within Appointments to configure:

- **Weekly business hours** and **holidays** — shared with your workspace-wide [Business Hours](/settings/business-hours) setting, so you only set this once.
- **Timezone** for displaying and calculating slots.
- **Services list** — the set of bookable services contacts can choose from.
- **Confirmation message template** — sent to a contact once their booking is confirmed.

## Public booking link

Each connected channel gets its own public, white-labeled **booking link** — a web page (not inside WhatsApp) where customers can self-book without needing to message you first. Copy this link from the channel's Appointments settings and share it anywhere (your website, email signature, social bio).

<Tip>
The public booking link runs on your own workspace domain, so it looks and feels like part of your business — not a third-party booking tool.
</Tip>
