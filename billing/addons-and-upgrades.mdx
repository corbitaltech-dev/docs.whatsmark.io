Sometimes you just need a bit more of one thing — more contacts, more AI tokens — without moving to an entirely different plan tier. That's what **Add-ons** and the **contact-limit upgrade** are for.

## Add-ons
Go to **Billing → Plan Add-ons** to browse what's available for your plan (for example, extra AI tokens or additional capacity for other quota-based features).

<Steps>
<Step title="Browse available add-ons">
  See what add-ons your plan supports and their pricing.
</Step>

<Step title="Add to cart">
  Add one or more add-ons to your cart, adjusting quantity as needed.
</Step>

<Step title="Check out">
  Complete payment for the cart — coupons can be applied here too (see [Payments & Invoices](/billing/payments-and-invoices)).
</Step>
</Steps>

Add-ons come in two kinds:

- **Recurring** add-ons renew automatically alongside your plan every cycle, until you remove them. You can change how many units you have at any time.
- **One-time** add-ons are a single purchase. They can't be adjusted — if you need more, buy another.

### Changing or removing an add-on

You can **remove** an add-on whenever you like. Removing it lowers your limit straight away, so make sure you're not still relying on the extra capacity.

<Warning>
If you **reduce the quantity** of a recurring add-on, your current usage has to fit inside the smaller limit. Say your plan includes 1,000 contacts and an add-on takes you to 2,000 — if you're storing 1,500 contacts, you'll need to delete some to get under 1,000 before you can drop that add-on's quantity.
</Warning>


## Contact-limit upgrade

If you're approaching your plan's contact limit but don't want to change your whole plan, you can raise it in one of two ways. Which one you use changes **when** the new limit takes effect:

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Buy a contact-limit add-on">
  Purchased from **Billing → Add-ons**, the same way as any other add-on:

  <Steps>
  <Step title="Preview the upgrade">
    See the cost of raising your contact limit before committing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pay for the upgrade">
    Pay via your usual payment method, or submit an offline payment for review.
  </Step>
  </Steps>

  This raises your contact limit **immediately**, within your current billing cycle, and the add-on then renews automatically alongside your plan on every future renewal — until you remove it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Change your contact limit with your billing cycle">
  You can also raise or lower your contact limit as part of upgrading or downgrading your billing cycle. Unlike buying an add-on, this change only takes effect from your **next billing cycle** — it doesn't apply to the cycle you're currently in. You can cancel a pending change any time before it takes effect.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
Check the [Feature Usage](/reporting/dashboard-and-usage#feature-usage-page) page to see exactly how close you are to your contact limit before deciding whether an add-on or a full plan upgrade makes more sense.
</Tip>
